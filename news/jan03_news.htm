4. UK/CHINESE CURRICULUM EXCHANGE

We would like to extend our appreciation and thanks to Actis and Promethean who funded school visits and a reception at the Conrad Hilton Hotel for the Chinese teachers at BETT03. It was particularly pleasing to have a mixed group of heads and senior teachers as well as classroom teachers.

Our thanks also go to the UK teachers, heads and the guides who hosted these visits. The schools involved were Cheam School in Reading, Blue Gate Fields Infants and Junior Schools in London, Waldegrave Girls School at Twickenham, and Chafford Hundred Campus in Essex. Each of these schools put extra time and effort into making the Chinese welcome and encouraged them to ask questions. This time was very much appreciated by the visitors.

We do not want these warm feelings of friendship and the opportunity for long term exchanges to dissolve away. Most of the teachers had made excellent contacts and had plans for projects. The MirandaNet Fellowship would like to give these plans a boost by providing a MirandaNet web page for the Mulan Community. WATCH THIS SPACE.

6. CONGRATULATIONS TO ALASTAIR WELLS AND KEITH PHIPPS - TEACHERS OF THE YEAR PRIZES @ BETT03

Becta run an annual award system called the 'ICT in Practice' awards. There are several award categories for Secondary, Primary, Management and tertiary college practitioners. The Teaching award seeks to recognise innovative or exemplary work which has impacted on pupils' learning. Nominees are nominated each year with a closing date at the end of July.

Nominations are shortlisted by judges. In the secondary category both Alastair Wells and Keith Phipps were shortlisted amongst eight to be visited in their schools. The shortlist was trimmed down to three. These people were then asked to make a presentation before a large panel of judges at a hotel near Becta headquarters in Coventry.

Alastair Wells was announced to be the joint winner of the secondary category and Keith Phipps was announced as the runner up. They received their awards at the BETT exhibition Olympia in London in January 2003.
