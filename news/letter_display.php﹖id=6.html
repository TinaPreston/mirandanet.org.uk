REMINDER TWO – MIND MAPPING SEMINAR AT IOE

MirandaNet seminar series

eLearning from a pupils' perspective
Using mind maps as a research instrument

Diane Mavers
Research Fellow, Manchester Metropolitan University

Date: Thursday 29 May 2003
Time: 12:00 - 2:00
Place: Committee Room 1, Institute of Education, 20 Bedford Way, London WC1H 0AL.

Image-based concept mapping was used as an innovative means of gaining insights into pupils’ thinking about ‘Computers in My World’ in the ImpaCT2 evaluation (1999-2002). ImpaCT2 was a major study commissioned by the DfES/Becta to explore the relationship between networked technologies and educational attainment/learning in the school and out of school environments.

This seminar focuses on the research instruments designed for pupils and what was learned from them. The Pupil Log, Special Report, Internet Questionnaire and peer interviewing provided data on how and why young people use computers, mainly in the home, both for schoolwork and leisure. The seminar also covers the pupils' views on ICT in society.

This free seminar and sandwich lunch is funded by EU Minerva SchoolScape@future project.

Please email Debra Cook to reserve your place.

UnITy – INTERNATIONAL CITIZENSHIP SITE

http://www.domex-unity.net

Given the state of international unrest, the MirandaNet Fellowship is increasingly keen to support you in developing International Citizenship projects.

Francis Howlett, John Cuthell, Mara Chrystie and Marion Scott Baker are working on a new site called UnITy which has private workspaces for pupils as well as a gallery of some of our projects in this area. Francis will give you a password for the closed areas where there are a lot of interesting exercises for pupils to do. We’d like your opinions.

In the gallery we are including projects in thinking skills, creativity, literacy, numeracy, computer literacy and key skills as well as the obvious links with cultural understanding, concern for world issues and a wide range of subject approaches to the topic of international citizenship.

We have the support of Charles Clarke who was very interested in our international outreach in the recent meeting Tina had with him. He wanted to know much more about how we manage international work in general. Tina hopes she made it clear that it has been the enthusiasm of our membership which has been the mainstay of our international reputation.

If you would like any of your projects featured on our new site let Francis have the link and we can make a connection. Sometimes it will be a link with your existing case study on the MirandaNet site as many of these are international in flavour. This invitation extends to all our colleagues abroad and all the MirandaNet chapters. It would be good to round up projects from the past and revisit them as there is so much good practice to share.

The time is ripe for action. Let us have some ideas for what we should do next in international citizenship. It’s time for the conciliators to move into a higher gear. That’s us!

MULTIMEDIA IN INTERNATIONAL EXCHANGES – GEOFFREY SCOTT-BAKER

At Cheam School in Berkshire, teachers have been experimenting with multimedia to communicate with fellow students in the USA and China. They are using the exchange of audio visual resources to bring the project to life. Extra visual and sound clues are being used to help students understand intuitively what life is like in another country.

Geoffrey Scott-Baker is an independent ICT Advisor and MirandaNet Fellow.

EUROPEAN POLICY INITIATIVE

Since January 1997, the European Experts' Network for Educational Technology (EENet) has been working as an informal network of expertise in the field of ICT in Education. The objective of EENet is the development, implementation and maintenance of a user-friendly online-database and observation system to promote, connect, consolidate, and disseminate knowledge about ICT-related transformations in European school education. On 7 March 2003, at a Foundation Meeting held in Düsseldorf (Germany), EENet became a registered Association. Irish MirandaNet Scholar Eileen Freeman was elected Vice-Chair of the Board. EENet has already started to invite new organisational members, details can be found at

http://www.eenet.org

or by contacting Eileen Freeman.
